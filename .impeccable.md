# AI Career Copilot for Disabled — Impeccable Design Context (PRO MAX)

## 1. Product Intent

A supportive AI-powered career copilot designed specifically for people with disabilities — helping them discover strengths, explore careers, build confidence, and successfully apply for jobs.

This is NOT a generic job board.
This is a guided journey toward meaningful employment.

---

## 2. Users

### Primary Users
People with disabilities across all age groups:

- Youth (18–30): students, fresh graduates, early career
- Mid-career professionals seeking growth or transition
- Career changers exploring new directions

### Accessibility Segments (CRITICAL)
Design must explicitly consider:

- Visual impairments (low vision, blindness)
- Motor impairments (limited mobility, tremors)
- Hearing impairments
- Cognitive differences (ADHD, dyslexia, anxiety)

### Context of Use
- At home (primary)
- Support centers
- On mobile during commute
- Possibly under stress or low confidence

---

## 3. Jobs-to-be-Done

Users come here to:

1. Understand themselves  
   → strengths, skills, preferences

2. Explore realistic career paths  
   → accessible, suitable, achievable

3. Prepare confidently  
   → CV, interviews, skill gaps

4. Take action  
   → apply, track, improve

---

## 4. Emotional Goals

Users should feel:

- Capable (not limited)
- Supported (not alone)
- Understood (not judged)
- Motivated (not overwhelmed)

NEVER make users feel:
- inferior
- pitied
- confused
- lost

---

## 5. Brand Personality

**3 words:** Warm — Professional — Empowering

### Tone Guidelines
- Encouraging, but never patronizing
- Professional, but never cold
- Supportive, but never overwhelming

### Core Principle
Focus on **capability, not disability**

---

## 6. Aesthetic Direction

### Theme
- Light mode (default): optimistic, accessible
- Dark mode: optional, comfortable

---

### Color System
- Primary: Blue (trust, clarity)
- Secondary: Warm neutrals (soft beige, warm gray)
- Accents: Gentle, not saturated

Avoid:
- Cold corporate blue gradients
- Hospital/medical palette
- Government-style UI

---

### Typography

Avoid: Inter (too neutral, techy)

Use:
- Display: Fraunces / Lora / DM Serif
- Body: DM Sans / Plus Jakarta Sans / Outfit

Tone:
- Human
- Warm
- Readable

---

### Layout Principles

- Generous whitespace
- Left-aligned content (preferred)
- Clear hierarchy
- Minimal visual clutter

Avoid:
- Card overload
- Dense dashboards
- Centered-heavy layouts

---

### Motion & Interaction

- Gentle, calm animations
- Smooth transitions
- Reinforce feedback (not decorative)

Respect:
- Reduced motion settings

---

## 7. Interaction Patterns (VERY IMPORTANT)

### Buttons
- Large, easy to click
- Clear labels (no ambiguity)
- High contrast

### Forms
- Step-by-step (never overwhelming)
- Inline guidance
- Smart defaults

### Errors
- Friendly, helpful, actionable
- Never alarming or technical

Example:
❌ "Invalid input"
✅ "Let’s try that again — this field needs a valid email"

---

### Navigation
- Simple, predictable
- No hidden complexity
- Always show "next step"

---

## 8. Accessibility Principles

Accessibility is NOT a feature — it is the foundation.

Always ensure:
- High contrast
- Keyboard navigation
- Screen reader compatibility
- Clear focus states
- Readable font sizes

Design for worst-case usability first.

---

## 9. Copywriting Style

- Short sentences
- Active voice
- Clear and direct
- Encouraging tone

Avoid:
- jargon
- corporate language
- long paragraphs

---

### Microcopy Examples

Instead of:
❌ "Submit application"

Use:
✅ "Apply for this job"

Instead of:
❌ "Error occurred"

Use:
✅ "Something didn’t work — let’s fix it together"

---

## 10. AI Behavior

The AI is a **career coach**, not a chatbot.

### Should:
- Give actionable advice
- Adapt to user ability
- Encourage progress
- Break tasks into steps

### Should NOT:
- Give generic answers
- Overwhelm users
- Sound robotic

Tone:
- Calm
- Supportive
- Clear

---

## 11. Progress & Motivation

Celebrate progress visually:

- Progress bars
- Completion states
- Encouraging messages

Make progress feel:
- visible
- achievable
- meaningful

---

## 12. Design Principles

1. Accessibility first, always  
2. Clarity over cleverness  
3. Warmth through restraint  
4. Support over automation  
5. Progress over perfection  

---

## 13. Anti-Patterns (STRICTLY AVOID)

- LinkedIn-style cold UI
- Government portal design
- Medical/clinical interfaces
- Overwhelming dashboards
- Generic AI chatbot UX
- Too many choices at once

---
